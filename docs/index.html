Spiral Chess

Spiral Chess is a turn-based placement game on a numbered grid, inspired by this Numberphile video, based on work by Jonas Karlsson. The two-knight game on a square grid generates the OEIS sequences A392177 and A392178.

The grid's cells are numbered along an spiral, progressing anti-clockwise outward, starting from the centre. The players take turns. On each turn, a player walks the spiral and places a piece on the first cell that is both empty and not attacked by any opponent's piece. Pieces are never removed. After N rounds each player has placed N pieces, each occupied cell colored by the respective player's color.

You can play with three generalizations to the base game. You can modify each player's starting position along the spiral (0 = centre). You can allow players to ignore threats from other players, enabling them to place their pieces in cells even if they are being attacked by those other players. And you can switch the grid from square to hexagonal, with the chess pieces generalized to hex moves.
