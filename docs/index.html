Spiral Chess

K players take turns. Each walks an outward square spiral from its own starting square and drops a piece on the first square not attacked by the other players' pieces. Based on OEIS A392177 / A392178. Pick the number of rounds, the players, their pieces, colors and starting squares, then press Run.

Spiral index over time

For each player, the index along the outward spiral (origin = 0) of the square chosen on each turn.

Piece movements

Every piece is an (m,n)-leaper, a generalization of the chess knight. From square (x,y) it jumps directly — over anything in between — to the up-to-8 squares (x±m, y±n) and (x±n, y±m). The knight is the (2,1)-leaper.
