Spiral Chess

K players take turns. Each walks an outward spiral from its own starting cell and drops a piece on the first cell not attacked by the other players' pieces. The square grid is based on OEIS A392177 / A392178; the hexagonal grid is the same game on a hex grid. Pick the grid, the number of rounds, the players, their pieces, colors and starting indices (position along the spiral, origin = 0), then press Run. A player that "ignores threat from" an opponent may place on a cell that opponent only attacks (no pieces are ever removed; occupancy still blocks everyone). The piece's leaper vector is shown after its name. Run, Randomize All and Reset All are at the top; most settings also have their own Randomize and/or Reset (Reset = back to default). Every Randomize/Reset runs immediately. Randomize All leaves the colors untouched. The view is cropped to just inside the least-advanced player's furthest shell. The page URL always encodes the current board — Copy link shares it. Pressing Enter in any field runs.
